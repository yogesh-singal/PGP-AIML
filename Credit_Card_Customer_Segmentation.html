Background:¶

AllLife Bank wants to focus on its credit card customer base in the next financial year. They have been advised by their marketing research team, that the penetration in the market can be improved. Based on this input, the Marketing team proposes to run personalised campaigns to target new customers as well as upsell to existing customers. Another insight from the market research was that the customers perceive the support services of the back poorly. Based on this, the Operations team wants to upgrade the service delivery model, to ensure that customers queries are resolved faster. Head of Marketing and Head of Delivery both decide to reach out to the Data Science team for help.

Objective:¶

To identify different segments in the existing customer based on their spending patterns as well as past interaction with the bank.

Key Questions:¶

  1. How many different segments of customers are there?
  2. How are these segments different from each other?
  3. What are your recommendations to the bank on how to better market to and service these customers? #### Data Description: Data is of various customers of a bank with their credit limit, the total number of credit cards the customer has, and different channels through which customer has contacted the bank for any queries, different channels include visiting the bank, online and through a call centre. #### Steps to follow:

#Finding optimal no. of clusters
from scipy.spatial.distance import cdist
from sklearn.cluster import KMeans

clusters=range(1,10)
meanDistortions=[]

for k in clusters:
    model=KMeans(n_clusters=k)
    model.fit(data)
    prediction=model.predict(data)
    meanDistortions.append(sum(np.min(cdist(data, model.cluster_centers_, 'euclidean'), axis=1)) / data.shape[0])


plt.plot(clusters, meanDistortions, 'bx-')
plt.xlabel('k')
plt.ylabel('Average distortion')
plt.title('Selecting k with the Elbow Method')

The segments differ from each other in :

  • Credit Limit (first segment has a mean credit limit of about 12,000 (low), the second about 34,000 (medium) and the third 141,000 (high))
  • Number of credit cards (first segment has a mean #credit cards of 2.4 (low), the second 5.5 (medium) and the third 8.7 (high))
  • Bank visits/online visits (first segment visits bank once, the second 3.5 times and the third less frequently - 0.6 times). However the lack of physical visits is compensated by higher number of online visits by the segment with the higher credit limit
  • Calls made (first segment calls more frequently - 6.9 times (high), the second 2 times (medium) and the third only once (low))
